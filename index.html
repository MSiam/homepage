About Me Publications

I am a Postdoctoral researcher working with Professors Richard Wildes and Konstantinos Derpanis in York University. I am currently focused on fewshot video segmentation and spatiotemporal models interpretability. I obtained my PhD in 2021 under Professor Martin Jagersand supervision working in vision for robotics. My thesis was focused on learning video segmentation from limited labelled data, and was nominated for the department award. I did internships in Huawei HiSilicon research (2019), Nvidia Deep Learning for Autonomous Vehicles Research (2018), and Valeo Vision Systems (2017). I was a member in a team of 4 in the KUKA Innovation Challenge 2018, where our team was one of the top 5 finalists. Previously I finished my Masters degree in Informatics from Nile University, Egypt, 2013. I also received an Honours BSc in Computer Science from Ainshams University, Egypt, 2010. Research Interests: Computer Vision, Deep Learning, Fewshot Learning, Video Object Segmentation
